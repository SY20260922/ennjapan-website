縁

ENN — fate, connection.
The invisible thread that binds the people meant to meet.

No other language has a word that fully captures it. We work with Japanese farmers and tea masters, and we call that bond en: not a network of contacts, but a foundation of relationships built on accumulated trust.

We chose this name as a standard to uphold. Every engagement we accept must create a new en — between the producer and the shelf, between the customer and the product, between two distant cultures.

Closing that distance is our work.

FEATURED PROJECT

Society 47 — Japan

A journey through 47 prefectures begins in Kyoto.

Society 47 is a collection created to carry the landscapes and cultures of Japan to the world. Like making one work for each prefecture, every product gathers the character of its soil, the intent of its producers, and techniques handed down over time.

Its first chapter, “Kyoto No.26,” is a starter collection themed on Kyoto’s matcha culture: matcha, a bamboo whisk, and stoneware bowls — a set for touching the aesthetic of the region through a single bowl of tea.

ORIGIN Uji, Kyoto Prefecture
THEME Made in Japan — an experience of matcha culture
PRODUCTION 100% made in Japan, from farm to final packaging
ROUTE UK & Europe
OPERATED BY ENN Japan × 1716 Industries
PROVENANCE

Proof, delivered with the product.

We do not believe the words “Made in Japan” are enough on their own. Who made it, where, and how — only by delivering that context does the true value come through. For Society 47 we are building a system that organizes origin, producer, and production records and provides them with the product.

Toward a new generation of quality assessment.

In time we aim to build our own quality-assessment platform using physical analysis and data — a market where quality is described not by impression alone, but by records and verification. It is an effort to protect producers and build consumer trust.
